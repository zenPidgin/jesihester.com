Michael Baker Website: Conversion from WordPress to Kentico

My Role: Designer, Front-End Developer

Year: 2015

Michael Baker purchased us in 2015, and almost immediately, we began the task of converting their website from WordPress to Kentico. My role was to identify where we could make design improvements and convert the unorganized WordPress theme into a leaner, performance-focused design.

The WordPress website was bloated with about 40,000 lines of CSS. I was able to reduce it to about 2500 lines. It may have a bit more now, as we have made quite a few updates since the last time I counted, but I considered that an achievement.

Alameda Restaurant Guide: Online Version

My Role: Front-End Developer

Year: 2015

This was a challenging project, built over Angular.JS. My role was to take the printed design of the approved Restaurant Guide, and implement it on-screen. One of the issues was repeating colors. In the printed version, no two sections of the same color ever touched. However, they were also not listed in alphabetical order, as they would be on the website. I had to do some color swapping here and there. I also needed to recreate the icons, and set up the parent-child relationships for the categories and sub-categories.

I had a lot of fun with mixins in this project and have the .scss files available for review if needed.
